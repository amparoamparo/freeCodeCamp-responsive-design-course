THE
SMITHS

The Smiths outside the Salford Lads Club in Manchester (1985).

The Smiths were an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1982. Their line-up comprised singer Morrissey, guitarist Johnny Marr, bassist Andy Rourke and drummer Mike Joyce.

They are regarded as one of the most important acts to emerge from the British independent music scene of the 1980s.

Some of their most popular songs include:

  • This Charming Man
  • There is a Light That Never Goes Out, and
  • How Soon is Now?.

Sadly, the band separated months before the release of their fourth and final studio album, 'Strangeways, Here We Come', in 1987.
